What is Fake News?

Fake news is false or misleading information presented as news. It is often created to influence people's opinions, provoke fear or anger, or generate clicks and money.

Types of Fake News:

  • Clickbait: Shocking or misleading headlines meant to attract attention.
  • Satire: Intended as humor or exaggeration but sometimes misunderstood as real news.
  • Propaganda: Information spread to promote a political cause or point of view.
  • Imposter Content: Fake sources or impersonating real news organizations.
  • Manipulated Content: Altered photos, videos, or statistics to deceive.

How to Spot Fake News:

  • Check the source — is it a reliable and well-known news site?
  • Read beyond the headline — headlines can be misleading.
  • Look for supporting evidence — are there quotes, links, or facts?
  • Check the date and author — is the story outdated or anonymous?
  • Use fact-checking websites like Snopes or FactCheck.org.

Always verify before sharing. Spreading fake news can mislead others and cause harm. Stay smart and skeptical online!
